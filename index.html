I am an Oceanography graduate specializing in marine climate hazards, hydrometeorological disasters, geospatial analysis, and marine pollution research. My academic and professional experience spans marine heatwave detection, multi-hazard coastal vulnerability assessment, flood susceptibility modeling (HEC-RAS, AHP, MCDA), and microplastic contamination studies in the Meghna estuary and Sandwip Channel.

I currently serve as a Research Assistant at the National Oceanographic and Maritime Institute (NOAMI), contributing to projects on the 2024 Eastern Bangladesh extreme flood event, multi-hazard coastal vulnerability mapping, and hydrological modeling for disaster preparedness.

Alongside my research experience, I have presented multiple posters and abstracts at international conferences (BUET IWFM, ICSF Sylhet Agricultural University, CVASU, BSMRMU, NSTU) and received multiple best-poster and academic awards. My long-term goal is to pursue graduate studies in marine or environmental science and contribute to climate-resilient coastal management and sustainable ocean governance.
