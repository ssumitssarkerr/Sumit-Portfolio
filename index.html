Interested in – Marine Heatwaves and Climate Variability • Remote Sensing of Ocean Surface Properties • Hydrological & Coastal Hazard Modelling • Marine Pollution and Ecotoxicology • GIS and Spatial Analysis.

Sumit Sarker is an oceanography graduate specializing in marine climate hazards, remote sensing, and environmental modelling. Growing up near the Meghna estuarine coastline, he developed a deep interest in coastal processes and ecosystem vulnerability. Sumit has conducted extensive research on marine heatwaves in the Bay of Bengal, flood susceptibility mapping using machine learning, and microplastics pollution in estuarine environments.

With multiple conference presentations, poster awards, and collaborations across environmental and GIS-based projects, he aims to contribute to advanced ocean–atmosphere research and pursue graduate studies in marine or environmental science. His long-term goal is to support climate-resilient coastal management and sustainable ocean resource governance.
